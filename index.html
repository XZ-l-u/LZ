About

I am a researcher with interdisciplinary training in accounting, business analytics, and information systems. My work examines the intersection of financial data integrity, database systems, and organizational decision-making—particularly in public sector and regulated environments.

My academic background spans accounting theory, quantitative methods, and applied database management. I hold an MBA in Business Intelligence and Business Analytics, a Graduate Certificate in Business Analytics and Artificial Intelligence, and a B.A. in Accounting. This combination allows me to approach research questions from both theoretical and technical perspectives.

"I am interested in understanding how organizations can ensure financial data accuracy across complex technological systems, and how database architecture decisions impact accounting accuracy and organizational trust."

Before focusing on research, I gained extensive practical experience in government finance, airport operations, and financial systems implementation. This professional background informs my research agenda and provides insight into real-world challenges facing organizations today.

Research Interests

My research interests center on the sociotechnical aspects of financial information systems:

  • Compensation Analysis Across Government Levels: Comparative analysis of compensation structures, benefits, and total rewards across federal, state, and local government positions—examining equity, retention, and workforce sustainability
  • Financial Data Integrity: How organizations maintain accuracy and trust in financial data across complex database systems and reporting pipelines
  • Government Budget Systems: Analysis of budgetary processes, allocation decisions, and financial planning systems across different levels of government
  • Database Architecture & Accounting: The relationship between database design decisions and financial reporting quality in ERP systems
  • Public Sector Financial Systems: Unique challenges of government accounting systems, data governance, and transparency
  • Human-Data Interaction: How accountants and analysts interact with database systems, and how design impacts error rates and data quality
  • AI & Automation in Financial Reporting: Implications of machine learning and automated systems for accounting accuracy, audit, and professional judgment
  • Ethics in Financial Data Systems: Responsibility, accountability, and ethical considerations in automated financial reporting

Working Papers & Projects

Compensation Equity Across Government Levels: A Comparative Analysis of Federal, State, and Local Government Employee Compensation
Working Paper | In Development
This study examines compensation structures across federal, state, and local government positions, analyzing total compensation packages including salary, benefits, pension systems, and other rewards. The research seeks to understand disparities in compensation for similar roles across government levels, implications for workforce retention and recruitment, and how these patterns affect public sector service delivery. Drawing on budget analysis experience and government financial data, this work addresses critical questions about equity and sustainability in public sector employment.
Budget Systems and Financial Decision-Making: Insights from State Government Operations
Working Paper
The Database-Accounting Gap: Understanding Technical-Professional Boundaries in Financial Systems
Research Proposal | Seeking Collaboration
This proposed study investigates the organizational challenges that arise when accounting professionals and database administrators must collaborate on financial systems. Preliminary fieldwork suggests that differing mental models, vocabularies, and priorities create systematic communication gaps that impact data quality and system effectiveness.
